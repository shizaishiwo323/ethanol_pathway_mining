Improved electrochemical conversion of CO to
2
multicarbon products by using molecular doping
Huali Wu1, Ji Li1,2, Kun Qi 1, Yang Zhang1, Eddy Petit1, Wensen Wang1, Valérie Flaud3, Nicolas Onofrio 1,
Bertrand Rebiere3, Lingqi Huang4, Chrystelle Salameh1, Luc Lajaunie 5,6, Philippe Miele 1,7 &

TheconversionofCO intodesirablemulticarbonproductsviatheelectrochemicalreduction
2
reaction holds promise to achieve a circular carbon economy. Here, we report a strategy in
which we modify the surface of bimetallic silver-copper catalyst with aromatic heterocycles
such as thiadiazole and triazole derivatives to increase the conversion of CO into hydro-
2
carbon molecules. By combining operando Raman and X-ray absorption spectroscopy with
electrocatalytic measurements and analysis of the reaction products, we identified that the
electron withdrawing nature of functional groups orients the reaction pathway towards the
production of C species (ethanol and ethylene) and enhances the reaction rate on the
2+
surfaceofthecatalystbyadjustingtheelectronicstateofsurfacecopperatoms.Asaresult,
we achieve a high Faradaic efficiency for the C 2+ formation of ≈80% and full-cell energy
efficiency of 20.3% with a specific current density of 261.4mAcm−2 for C 2+ products.
